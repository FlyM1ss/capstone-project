## 5. SECURITY & COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS

### Regulatory Frameworks

#### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- **Scope**: Legal framework created by EU to protect personal data and privacy
- **Applicability**: Any organization processing personal data of EU/EEA individuals
- **Requirements**: Proper handling, processing, and storage of personal data
- **Security**: Appropriate safeguards to ensure data integrity and confidentiality (encryption, anonymization)

#### SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2)
- **Nature**: Voluntary framework developed by American Institute of CPAs
- **Purpose**: Demonstrate commitment to data security and privacy
- **Trust Service Criteria**:
  1. **Security**: Protection against unauthorized access
  2. **Availability**: Systems operational and accessible
  3. **Processing Integrity**: Accurate and timely data processing
  4. **Confidentiality**: Protecting sensitive data
  5. **Privacy**: Proper handling of PII

**Multi-Framework Compliance**: Organizations can (and should) comply with both GDPR and SOC 2 simultaneously, particularly when handling EU resident data.

**Sources**:
- [GDPR Local - Compliance Guide](https://gdprlocal.com/navigating-compliance-gdpr-and-soc-2-compared/)
- [Scytale - GDPR vs SOC 2](https://scytale.ai/question/what-are-the-key-differences-between-gdpr-and-soc-2-compliance/)

### Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

**Definition**: Security method granting system access based on job role, not individual users

**Key Components**:
- **Roles**: Job titles with defined permission sets (Manager vs. Clerk)
- **Permissions**: Specific actions allowed (view-only vs. full edit access)
- **Scalability**: Ideal for enterprises and regulated industries

**Why Essential for Enterprise Search**:
- Ensures employees find what they need while preventing unauthorized access
- Limits information visibility based on job role
- Critical for professional services with confidential client data

**Enterprise Value**: Sophisticated RBAC authorization models enable higher average selling prices for enterprise customers vs. basic offerings.

**Sources**:
- [Guru - RBAC Comprehensive Guide](https://www.getguru.com/reference/role-based-access-control)
- [Akooda - RBAC in Enterprise Search](https://www.akooda.co/blog/role-based-access-control-in-enterprise-search)
- [Enterprise Ready - RBAC Guide](https://www.enterpriseready.io/features/role-based-access-control/)

### Security Requirements for Internal Tools

**Key Considerations**:
1. **Data Encryption**: At rest and in transit
2. **Access Controls**: Granular permissions and role-based access
3. **Audit Trails**: Comprehensive logging of access and usage
4. **Data Residency**: Compliance with geographic data storage requirements
5. **Privacy by Design**: Built-in privacy protections, not retrofitted
6. **Client Data Separation**: Strict isolation from internal knowledge
7. **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication and SSO integration
8. **Compliance**: GDPR, SOC 2, ISO 27001 adherence

**Professional Services Context**: Given consulting firms handle highly sensitive client information, internal search tools must maintain strict separation between internal knowledge and client data while providing seamless access to appropriate internal resources.
